Interpretation Guide
How to read this report

A plain-English guide to the model, the terminology, and how to act on the outputs. No prior knowledge of SuperCat required.

🏥

Health Score (0–100)

How well an account is currently using the platform. Measures engagement (login activity), adoption (catalog usage), value delivery (orders and results), operational health (setup quality), and trajectory (trend direction). A score of 65+ is Healthy. Below 50 needs attention. Below 35 is At Risk or Critical.

📈

Growth Score (0–100)

How much near-term expansion opportunity exists. Measures whether the account has outgrown their bundle (upgrade signal), whether they are missing useful features (feature gap), how much room they have to grow (customer headroom), and how far they lag similar companies (peer benchmark gap). 60+ = Ready to grow.

🎯

Action Classification

The model's recommended action: Expand = go sell now. Maintain = stable, standard cadence. Stabilize First = fix health before pitching growth. Intervene = health too low, needs immediate attention. These are directional recommendations — not automated decisions.

👥

Peer Benchmark

Each account is compared to a cohort of similar companies — same industry vertical and same software bundle. High confidence = 8+ well-matched peers. Low confidence = smaller or fallback cohort. Unavailable = no comparable peers exist. Peer context is one input into the growth score, not a standalone verdict.

📦

Software Bundles

iPad-only: Mobile catalog app. iPad+Catalog: Mobile + online B2B catalog website. iPad+Catalog+Cart: Adds online ordering. iPad+Catalog+Portal: Adds a rep/dealer sales portal. Full: All modules. Higher bundles = more features and typically more growth opportunity.

⚠️

Churn Risk & Expansion Flags

Churn Risk: Active disengagement signals. Immediate = respond within days. Monitored = plan intervention within weeks. Do not pitch growth to churn-risk accounts. Expansion Ready: Growth score crossed the Ready threshold — a proactive commercial conversation is warranted.

Classification guide — what each label means and what to do

✦ Expand

This account is healthy and commercially actionable now. Health is Healthy or Thriving, and growth score has crossed the Ready threshold (≥60), typically driven by a bundle upgrade signal. This means usage patterns suggest the account has outgrown their current tier.

  • Prioritize for proactive commercial conversation
  • Bundle upgrade is the primary pitch angle
  • Do not delay — growth signals are time-sensitive
  • These accounts can absorb expansion because health is stable

◆ Maintain

This account is in good health but limited near-term growth signal. No urgent action needed. Standard engagement cadence is appropriate. Growth score may be Developing or Not Ready.

  • Standard quarterly check-in cadence
  • Monitor trajectory for early warning of decline
  • Not a priority for outbound growth motion today
  • If growth score rises to Ready, re-evaluate for Expand

▲ Stabilize First

The growth signal is real, but the health foundation is shaky. Growth score is Ready (≥60) but health is Watch or below. Expanding before stabilizing often accelerates churn rather than preventing it.

  • Fix the health issue before opening a growth conversation
  • Identify the failing health dimension in the detail view
  • Once health recovers, this account moves to Expand
  • High-value target — worth investing in health recovery

■ Intervene

Health is too low for any growth motion. The account needs direct attention. May involve disengagement, adoption collapse, operational issues, or trajectory crash. Churn risk flags here are urgent.

  • Assign an owner and create an intervention plan immediately
  • Identify the primary failing dimension in the detail view
  • Do not pitch growth products to an account in crisis
  • If RM-2 modifier is present, treat as highest priority

Method Notes
Deeper model context

For curious readers. Expand any section. The main report is fully readable without this layer.

Health Score: Five Dimensions Explained
Engagement (20% weight)
How actively users log into the platform. Measured by login frequency, session count, and activity density. Low engagement (<40) indicates the platform is not part of daily workflows — a leading indicator of future churn. A score of 0 means no measurable login activity in the scoring window.
Adoption (25% weight)
Depth and breadth of catalog and feature usage. Measures whether users actively browse, present, and use the catalog. A score below 30 suggests the account treats the platform as a static asset rather than an active sales tool.
Value Delivery (25% weight)
Whether the account generates tangible outcomes — order volume, order activity rates, presentation activity, and the primary value metric specific to their bundle. A score of 0 means no measurable order or presentation activity. Often the most critical health dimension.
Operational Health (15% weight)
Quality of the account's platform setup — catalog completeness, product data hygiene, configuration correctness. Poor operational health often explains poor adoption and value delivery scores.
Trajectory (15% weight)
The directional trend of health activity over recent periods. A score of 0 means platform activity has dropped significantly — the strongest early warning signal for churn. A score of 100 with low other dimensions may indicate recent onboarding or recovery.
Growth Score: Four Components Explained
Bundle Upgrade Signal
Whether the account is using the platform in ways that suggest they have outgrown their current bundle. High signal (75–100) = usage is consistent with a higher tier. This is the primary commercial conversation starter for Expand-classified accounts.
Feature Gap Score
How much untapped value exists through features the account is not using. High feature gap often indicates enablement opportunities more than upsell. A score of 0 means they are already using all relevant features, or the model could not identify a clear gap.
Customer Headroom Score
Estimated remaining growth capacity within the existing relationship — based on account size, industry, catalog coverage, and utilization rates. High headroom (70+) means meaningfully underutilizing relative to potential.
Peer Benchmark Gap
The composite distance between this account's metrics and its peer cohort median. A high gap (80–100) means significantly underperforming versus similar companies — signaling both risk and opportunity. When benchmark confidence is low or unavailable, this component uses a fallback estimate and should be interpreted cautiously.
Peer Benchmark: How Cohorts Are Built and What Confidence Means

Peer groups are constructed by matching accounts on industry vertical and software bundle (Tier 1 — most precise). If no Tier 1 group exists, the system falls back to vertical-only (Tier 2), bundle-only (Tier 3), or a global fallback (Tier 4).

  • High confidence — Tier 1 cohort with 8+ matched peers. Comparisons are robust and meaningful.
  • Medium confidence — Tier 1 with 4–7 peers, or broader vertical match. Directionally useful.
  • Low confidence — Tier 2 or Tier 3 fallback. Less homogeneous cohort. Use as loose reference.
  • None / Unavailable — No eligible peers found. Benchmark gap is fallback-estimated. Pioneer Morton is the only such account in this run.

Scoring status and benchmark confidence are different things. Scoring status = whether all scoring inputs were available. Benchmark confidence = quality of the peer comparison. An account can be complete-scored with low benchmark confidence.

Risk Modifiers (RM codes) — What They Mean

Risk modifiers are rule-based adjustments triggered when an account meets specific threshold criteria signaling elevated risk beyond what the standard formula captures. They are not manual overrides.

RM-2: Critical Engagement + Value Collapse
Applied when an account simultaneously has critically low engagement AND near-zero value delivery — indicating the platform is essentially unused. RM-2 triggers a downward health score adjustment and escalates churn risk to “immediate.” Accounts with RM-2 require urgent human-led intervention. In this run: Oly Studio, Highland House, Philip Whitney.

Other modifier codes (RM-1, RM-3, etc.) exist in the model but did not trigger in this run. Only RM-2 appears in the April 3, 2026 data.
