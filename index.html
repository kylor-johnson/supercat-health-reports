How to Read This Report

A concise guide to the model, the scoring system, peer benchmarking, and how to interpret reliability signals.

🏥

Health Score (0–100)

Measures current platform state across five dimensions: Engagement, Adoption, Value Delivery, Operational Health, and Trajectory. Higher = healthier. Not forward-looking — this is how the platform is performing today.

📈

Growth Score (0–100)

Measures credible upside signals: bundle upgrade potential, feature gaps that represent expansion paths, customer headroom, and peer benchmark position. Higher = more near-term growth levers available.

🎯

Classification

Combines Health + Growth into an action orientation. Expand = healthy + ready. Maintain = healthy + limited upside. Stabilize First = upside exists but health needs work. Intervene = current state needs attention.

👥

Peer Benchmarking

Each org is compared to a cohort of similar accounts. Tier 1 = vertical × bundle match. Tier 2 = vertical only. Tier 3 = bundle only (valid, not a failure). Tier 4 = no viable cohort. Confidence (high / medium / low) reflects cohort quality.

⚠️

Partial Scoring

3 orgs in this run are scored partial — they have missing total_users which affects Engagement precision. Scores are still directionally valid and should be used as decision support. They are not broken or excluded.

🔢

peer_group_n

peer_group_n counts steady-state peers only — orgs with ≥1 year tenure. Ramping orgs are assigned to a cohort but excluded from the peer count. A small n means wider variance in percentile ranks. Treat cautiously when n < 5.

Classification Logic ?

Expand

Health ≥ 60 and Growth ≥ 50. The platform is in good shape and there are credible signals to pursue expansion — bundle upgrade, feature adoption, or peer benchmark gap.

Maintain

Health ≥ 60 but Growth < 50. The platform is healthy but no major near-term growth lever is identified. Keep engagement high and monitor for emerging opportunities.

Stabilize First

Health < 60 but Growth ≥ 50. Meaningful upside signals exist but the health foundation isn't ready to support them. Fix platform health before acting on growth triggers.

Intervene

Health < 60 and Growth < 50. Both health and growth signals require attention. Prioritize diagnosis and corrective action before any expansion conversation.

Peer Confidence Guide ?

high
Tier 1 cohort (vertical × bundle) with ≥8 steady-state peers. Percentile ranks are reliable. Use for direct comparison claims.
medium
Tier 1 cohort with 5–7 steady-state peers. Meaningful as trend direction; specific values should be treated directionally.
low
Tier 2 or Tier 3 fallback, or ramping org. Benchmark is still useful as a directional signal. Tier 3 is a valid bundle-level comparison — not failure.
none
Tier 4 — no viable cohort at any level. Benchmark section omitted.
