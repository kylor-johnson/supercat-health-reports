How to Read This Report

A concise orientation before you dive in. Two scores, four classifications, one key question per account.

🏥

Health Score (0–100)

Current platform state across five dimensions: Engagement, Adoption, Value Delivery, Operational Health, and Trajectory. ≥60 = platform is working. Backward-looking — reflects today, not a forecast.

📈

Growth Score (0–100)

Near-term expansion signals: bundle upgrade readiness, feature gap, customer headroom (commercial growth within current base), and peer benchmark gap. ≥50 = real expansion levers exist. Measures current opportunity signals, not future predictions.

🎯

The 2×2 Classification

Expand: Health ≥60 + Growth ≥50. Act now.
Maintain: Health ≥60 + Growth <50. Stable. Monitor.
Stabilize First: Health <60 + Growth ≥50. Upside exists — repair health first.
Intervene: Health <60 + Growth <50. Both need attention.

👥

Peer Benchmarking

Each org compared to similar accounts. Tier 1 = vertical × bundle. Tier 2 = vertical only. Tier 3 = bundle only (valid, not a failure). Confidence: high / medium / low. Click into any account for full peer context and peer list.

⚠️

Churn Risk & Modifiers

RM-1 = ARR >$5K + zero logins. RM-2 = severe value collapse (>60% drop). RM-3 = elevated support escalations. RM-4 = data staleness. When any modifier fires, churn_risk = true and Health is capped. Severity: immediate / near-term / monitored.

Model Reference & Interpretation Guide

Full scoring logic, classification definitions, peer confidence guide, and data quality signals.

Classification Logic

Expand

Health ≥60 and Growth ≥50. Platform is in good shape and credible expansion signals exist — bundle upgrade, feature adoption, or peer benchmark gap. Act on growth while protecting health.

Maintain

Health ≥60 but Growth <50. Platform is healthy but no near-term expansion trigger identified. Keep engagement high, monitor for emerging signals. No urgent action required.

Stabilize First

Health <60 but Growth ≥50. Real upside exists — expansion potential is present. But the health foundation must be repaired first. Stabilize First ≠ Intervene: this account has a credible growth path waiting behind a health repair gate.

Intervene

Health <60 and Growth <50. Both health and growth need attention. Diagnose, then stabilize. Not all Intervene accounts are equal — some are immediate save cases (RM fired), some are repair cases, some are monitor-tier. Use the detail panel to assess urgency.

Peer Confidence Guide

high
Tier 1 cohort (vertical × bundle) with ≥8 steady-state peers. Percentile ranks are reliable. Use for direct comparison claims.
medium
Tier 1 with 5–7 peers. Meaningful and the right cohort; treat specific percentile values as directional.
low
Tier 2 or Tier 3 fallback, or ramping account. Useful as directional signal. Tier 3 is valid (often n=50+) — not a failure state.
none
Tier 4 — no viable peer cohort. Benchmark section omitted for this account.

Health Dimensions

Engagement

Active rep logins and usage in 90 days. Most direct usage signal — low engagement is often the upstream cause of all other health gaps.

Adoption

Breadth of feature utilization. Low = narrow platform use. High = team engaged across catalog, ordering, and portal tools.

Value Delivery

Tangible business outcomes: orders, email/catalog workflows, portal engagement. Closest proxy to “is eCat generating ROI?”

Operational Health

Data freshness, import hygiene, catalog completeness. Stale or incomplete data degrades the catalog experience for reps and buyers.

Trajectory

Current 90d vs prior 90d. 100 = improving. 0 = declining. A leading indicator — declining trajectory often precedes deeper disengagement.
